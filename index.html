Cholera in Haiti and the International Cover-up

Historical cases show that there have been no cases of cholera in Haiti since the 1800s, but in October 2010, Haitians living less than two miles from a UN camp began dying of the disease.

Since then, scientific studies, video documentation and Haitians have come forward with evidence to substantiate the claim that the UN introduced the disease to earthquake-ravaged Haiti.

I would like to make a data visualization of cholera's journey, from an outbreak in mid-west Nepal, to the capital, Kathmandu, up on board a 40-hour flight that stopped in Karachi, the Azores and then in Haiti where it fanned out. It has since moved to the Dominican Republic, Venezuela, Cuba and Florida.
